# Executive HR Analytics & Attrition Insights Report

This report outlines the analytical findings and business recommendations derived from the employee attrition modeling. Unexpected turnover carries high institutional costs (recruitment, onboarding, lost productivity). Below is a summary of the major structural risk factors identified and our strategic recommendations to mitigate them.

---

## 1. Primary Turnover Drivers

The Random Forest model identified key predictors that dictate whether an employee is likely to leave. Below are the primary drivers:

### A. Overtime Workload (Burnout Indicator)
- **Finding**: Employees working overtime leave at more than **double** the rate of those who do not work overtime. 
- **Business Context**: Overtime is the strongest predictor of attrition. This suggests that workload saturation and burnout are primary causes of exit.

### B. Compensation Competitive Gap
- **Finding**: Attrition is heavily concentrated among employees with low monthly incomes (specifically under $4,500/month). 
- **Business Context**: Employees leaving the company have a median income significantly below the overall company average. This indicates that junior staff or under-compensated employees are actively seeking external salary matches.

### C. Early Career Churn (Tenure & Age)
- **Finding**: Employees with less than 2 years of tenure at the company, and younger demographics (under 30), show a significantly higher rate of departure.
- **Business Context**: The first 18-24 months represent a critical window. If career development or team integration is weak, early-stage employees leave before providing a return on onboarding costs.

### D. Job & Environmental Satisfaction
- **Finding**: Low satisfaction scores (1 and 2 on a 4-point scale) account for a disproportionate volume of voluntary exits.
- **Business Context**: Internal friction, poor manager relationships, or stressful environments directly drive exit decisions independent of compensation.

---

## 2. Recommended Action Plans

To lower overall attrition, HR managers should execute these targeted programs:

### I. Implement Overtime Thresholds & Guardrails
- **Action**: Monitor weekly overtime hours per employee. Set up alert protocols in team scheduling when overtime exceeds 10 hours/week.
- **Outcome**: Lower burnout rates and reduce flight risk among critical operational roles.

### II. Target Salary Adjustment Bands
- **Action**: Conduct a market review of salaries for roles under the $4,500 monthly threshold. Implement structural pay adjustments for high-performers to match local industry benchmarks.
- **Outcome**: Stabilize junior and entry-level staff turnover.

### III. Introduce Early-Tenure Mentorship Programs
- **Action**: Build a structured "First-Year Integration" roadmap. Pair new hires with senior team mentors outside their direct reporting line, and hold mandatory 30, 90, and 180-day pulse check-ins.
- **Outcome**: Boost retention in the critical first 2 years of employment.

### IV. Regular Management Feedback & Culture Audits
- **Action**: Conduct anonymous bi-annual department health surveys. For teams scoring low on Job or Environment satisfaction, run targeted team-building workshops or manager training programs.
- **Outcome**: Identify and resolve management friction before it triggers exits.
